The District Magistrate Nainital, Uttarakhand

Subject: Appeal for taking immediate action against the installation of an illegal bore well in Village Satoli, Patti Malli Kutoli, District Nainital located at Khet No. 1300 Khata No. 108 being used for commercial purposes in complete defiance of the law

This petition is with reference to the above-mentioned subject matter. 

The aforementioned illegal bore well is in the name of one Vipin Chand Pant, a resident of Bhowali Village who was granted permission for the borewell in the Satoli area 10 years back, even when he was not a resident of Satoli and had no property in the location in question. Moreover, he still does not own any residential property in the site location. It is important to mention here that even after getting the permission, he did not do anything in order to use the permission. Now, after the lapse of the permission, he again applied for fresh permission and he was permitted within 24 hours by the Sub-divisional officer, Pragana Magistrate, without looking into the ground realities and the change in circumstances in these 10 years. The permission was given on 09.02.2021 without following the procedure of consulting the villagers, inspection, and verification from a special hydrologist, which are important to ascertain the impact of the permission on the Ecology. The borewell is now operational and it is pertinent to mention here that its water is being diverted by the builders for commercial use (construction of high-end villas) via an illegal water line, connecting the borewell with the construction site, hundreds of meters away.

Needless to say, groundwater abstraction is highly regulated by law, given the devastating impact, it has on the environment. Misuse of groundwater is a punishable offense.

https://hilansh.com/news/villagers-of-satoli-gathered-against-the-loot-of-water-in-uttarakhands-nainital-district

It is a well-known fact that digging borewells in ecologically sensitive hill areas is a recipe for disaster. Since hilly areas do not have a water table under the surface, bore wells draw on all available water channels. The most disturbing aspect of a boring activity in the hills is that it taps into or cuts through any of the water channels and sucks out the water from the capillaries, ultimately leading to drying up of the water source of villages at lower altitudes.

https://devbhoomisamachaar.com/index.php/2021/09/20/a-civil-movement-against-environmental-destruction/

Courts have held that it is the legal obligation of the state to act against those who violate the law and misuse the natural resources of the country.
